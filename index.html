Trevor Tran

Aspiring Neurologist

If the world were created where everyone was the same, there would be no problems and everything would be boring. Everyone has or had experienced difficulties and differences throughout their lives. I had the chance to experience a different life than most. I have an older brother who had been diagnosed with special needs when we both were little. After finding out about this diagnosis and what it meant, I have devoted myself to helping and caring for my brother and anyone else who needs medical help.

Ever since I was five, I was always curious. I wondered why my brother acted the way he did. From his inability to speak, not understanding anything anyone says, not knowing how to take care of himself, and many more disabilities, I wanted to find out why. As I continued to grow, I found out he was diagnosed with autism and that there had been no cure found. I wanted to find a cure right then and there, but I also knew I was not qualified or of age at the time, so I dedicated my time to studying hard as well as helping care for my brother. In high school, I noticed that he had and still has episodes of epilepsy. From then on, I would continue to listen to the doctors that he had and learn about the ways to spot, treat, and understand the occurrence of epilepsy.

I aspire to become a neurologist. I found it interesting to study the mind and help those with disorders, such as movement, memory, and epilepsy. I would love to be able to help those who are diagnosed with such disorders just like my brother. I hope to make a big impact with my contribution to neurology in the future.
